A study on awareness about cyber-crime among students

The research paper "A Study on Awareness About Cybercrime Among College Students" by Arbaz Sayyad et al., published in the International Journal of Novel Research and Development, examines college students' awareness of cybercrime. The study assesses students' knowledge and vulnerabilities and suggests ways to enhance awareness. Using data from 50 students in Narhe, Pune, the analysis shows significant awareness but identifies areas needing improvement, such as online safety practices and crime reporting. The paper recommends comprehensive awareness programs and legal measures to address these issues. Keywords include Cyber-Crime, Cyber Security, IT Superpower, IT Act, and Awareness.

A study towards customer perception on E-commerce

The research paper "A Study Towards Customer Perception on E-Commerce" by Arbaz A. Sayyad et al., published in the International Journal of Novel Research and Development, explores customer perceptions of e-commerce in Narhe-Pune. The study aims to understand customer preferences for online versus offline shopping, factors influencing these preferences, and the risks associated with e-commerce. Using data from 40 respondents, the findings show a high satisfaction rate with online shopping, driven primarily by economic factors and convenience. However, issues such as return policies and quality concerns remain. The study suggests improvements in security, return processes, and product quality to enhance the e-commerce experience.
